Grooming

Question 1: Name 3 types of scissors used in the grooming salon and state a use for each.

Click below to see the answer

  • Thinning Scissors: Used for blending and reducing bulk in the fur.
  • Curved Scissors: Helps shape rounded areas like paws and faces.
  • Straight Scissors: Used for general trimming and shaping.

Question 2: When using a clipper, what must you consider? Give 4 consideration points.

Click below to see the answer

  • Ensure the blade is clean and lubricated.
  • Choose the right blade number for the coat type.
  • Regularly check if the blade is getting too hot to prevent burns.
  • Keep the dog’s skin taut to avoid accidental cuts.

Question 3: When washing a dog, what advise would you give about using the shower head? Give 4 points.

Click below to see the answer

  • Use lukewarm water for comfort.
  • Adjust the water pressure to avoid scaring the dog.
  • Avoid directing water into the dog’s eyes and ears.
  • Wet the dog gradually to reduce stress.

Question 4: How long does mixed shampoo keep for (at what point must it be disposed of?).

Click below to see the answer

Mixed shampoo should be used within 24 hours to prevent bacterial growth.

Question 7: What signs or signals might a dog give you when they are getting stressed during the grooming process? (identify 4 signals/signs).

Click below to see the answer

  • Excessive panting.
  • Trembling or cowering.
  • Repetitive nose licking.
  • Trying to escape or pull away.

Question 8: What would you do if a dog is getting stresses during the grooming process?.

Click below to see the answer

  • Pause the grooming session and give the dog a break.
  • Use a calming voice and gentle handling.
  • Offer treats or reassurance to create a positive experience.
  • Adjust your approach, using slower and softer movements.

Question 9: What steps would you take if you cut the quick while clipping a dog's nails?.

Click below to see the answer

Grab some styptic powder and apply it to a cotton bud and hold the powder to the bleeding part of the nail for about 10 seconds at least. This will stop the bleeding.

Question 10: Why should you clip a dog's nails after you have bathed them?.

Click below to see the answer

Because after the bath the nail are softer and easy to cut, its reduces the risk of splitting or cracking your nails. Also clean paws make it easy to see the quick.

Click below to see the answer
